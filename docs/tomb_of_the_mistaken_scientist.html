the tomb of the mistaken scientist

short

Perfectly intelligent, diligent and capable scientists can, through no fault of their own, devote their lives to developing and testing theories/hypotheses that are completely wrong. The tomb of the mistaken scientists is a poetic attempt to value their efforts and prevent the lionising of those scientists who get things right (by our lights) and dismissing those who got things wrong as necessarily*No Virginia, this is not a commitment to modal realism second rate.

main

There is a tendency, particularly among those who believe there is a scientific method, to lionize successful scientists such as Einstein, Newton, Snow*See tulodziecki2021-howNotToThinkAboutTheoryChange

etc., and at least in the popular press they are described as geniuses. The tacit (and sometimes not so tacit) claim is that the scientists who opposed them were less than genius's

In much the same way that we acknowledge the sacrifice of the unknown soldier, we might acknowledge the sacrifice of scientists who devoted their lives (or at least much of their time) to seeking evidence for false theories.

An admittedly foolish idea, for making vivid the lack of a scientific method and the lack of guarantee that being smarter/more diligent/following the scientific method reliably leads to truth.
