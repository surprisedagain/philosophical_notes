On Bias

short

Bias is supposed to be an epistemic defect which is inescapable. I suspect that this is a hangover

related to

foundationalism, bayesianism

main

Bias appears to be one of the few instances where ought does not imply can. On the one hand bias is a defect, it marks a departure from some kind of ideal rationality, the forming of an belief that is not justified by the evidence (or failing to form a belief that is) perhaps due to ones preferences desires or perhaps cherished opinions.

examples

Aja likes Pierson and thinks very highly of them. Various information comes to light (perhaps reports from other people) that Pierson has done something that Aja generally thinks is reprehensible. Say Pierson cheated on a test, or stole a candy from a baby. The evidence is clearcut. However, rather than form the belief that Pierson has done something reprehensible Aja claims the evidence is not decisive, going to lengths to invent other implausible explanations of the evidence (a bird flew down, took the candy and dropped it in Pierson's hand) rather than form the conclusion that their friend has done something wrong. (Which would lead Aja to think less of Pierson, which Aja would find unpleasant.)
