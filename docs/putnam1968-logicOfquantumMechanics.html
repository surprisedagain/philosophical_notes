§1 overthrow of euclidean geometry

geometry as idealisation vs physical reality

the claim that straight lines which are equidistant for some length are equidistant for their entire length is intuitively necessary. (This distance between parallel lines is constant.)

To deny this principle is just as self-contradictory as to claim that an object is both red and green, both married and a bachelor.

And yet General Relativity does just this!
