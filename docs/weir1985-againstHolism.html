sequential summary

Does not carefully distinguish between semantic holism and confirmation holism. talks of linguistic holism instead.

Talks of understanding a sentence and grasping a proposition. Holism is the doctrine that one cannot understand a sentence without understanding every sentence of the langauge Author judges such holism implausible.

Atomism (i.e. any word or sentence can be understood independent of any other word or sentence whatsoever) judged equally implausible:

Natural suggestion to explore whether there is some boundary less that the whole language which must be understood in order to understand a [given] sentence. Apparent objective of the first two sections to discredit this suggestion.

OK, now distinguishes the Duhem-Quine thesis as an alternate form of holism. This may well be what I call confirmation holism.
