6

types of democracy

  1. many who live by their labour, they have no leisure - must rule by law When the class of husbandmen and of those who possess moderate fortunes have the supreme power, the government is administered according to law. For the citizens being compelled to live by their labour have no leisure; and so they set up the authority of the law, and attend assemblies only when necessary.
  2. everyone to whose birth there is no eligible objection: in such a democracy the supreme power is vested in the laws, because the state has no means of paying the citizens.
  3. all freemen have a right to share in government but do not actually share. Here again the law must rule
  4. leisured underclass - the poor receive pay. the state is governed by the poor, who are a majority, and not by the laws.
