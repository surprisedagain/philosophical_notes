notes on first reading

Alternative theories

received view

dreams are (or at least involve) experiences that occur during sleep There are experiences there then that may be forgotten upon waking or soon afterwards.

dennett's fantasy projection of received view

there are two processes occurring during sleep when one is dreaming.

  1. presentation: neural events that represent the events in the dream. (which presumably give rise to experiences)
  2. A process of recording those events into memory.

later (fp. 7) a third process is added:

  1. composition(/generation?) of the dream experiences
Malcolm's view

If I call it, its a strike!
People have authority over their dream contents. A more than somewhat verificationist view seemingly reminiscent of Wittgenstein's account. (and hence wrong) Hence there is nothing occurring when you are asleep (otherwise you could be wrong about it).

production upon waking

This view is invented purely to solve the #precognition problem.

dreams are composed and presented very fast in the interval between bang, bump, or buzz and full consciousness, with some short delay system postponing the full "perception" of the noise in the dream until the presentation of the narrative is ready for it.
cassette theory

This is also introduced to solve the #precognition problem

perhaps there is a "library" in the brain of undreamed dreams with various indexed endings, and the bang or bump or buzz has the effect of retrieving an appropriate dream and inserting it, cassette-like, in the memory mechanism.

On the cassette view we never dream our dream at all, only spuriously recall them upon waking. The presentation (process (1) above) is ommitted altogether. Various version of the cassette theory some of these dream cassettes might be innate, some might be composed at various times by some unconscious process.

REM composition cassette theory

On this theory dream memories are produced just the way the received theory says they are, except for one crucial thing: the process of dream-memory production is entirely unconscious, involves no awareness or experiencing at all. The composition stage occurs when, according to the received view, the presentation process is thought to occur (basically during REM sleep).

The (sole?) superiority of this view is that it accounts for the #precognition problem

dennett's view

evidence

eye movements: gross features of REM eye movements sometimes match the content. Looking left and right - later reported dreaming of watching a tennis match. Looking up and down - later reported dreaming of throwing basketballs.

precognition problem: this is the key bit of evidence to explain, and the chief motivation for the dreams dont occur while sleeping but may occur upon waking (or perhaps the recollection is when you are doing the dream activity).

An alarm clock, sudden noise, drip of water, is incorporated into a dream at its climax and the dreamer wakes up to find that it is real. How could that (lengthy?) dream have made up all that if I were experiencing it over the time it seemd to take and then coincide with that waking-up real world stimulus/event.

If the terminal events in these dreams are strongly prepared for by the narrative, if they do not consist of radically juxtaposed turns in the narrative (for example, the goat turns into a telephone and starts ringing), then the composition process must have been directed by something having "knowledge" of the future.
(fp. 9)
my own dream

Twice now I have had a dream when I am interacting with an electronic device. Once it was my phone, once it was something else I cannot now recall. But it was making a noise (not unpleasant or loud merely annoying) and I turned it off in the normal way. It failed, repeatedly try to turn off the device and become frustrated at its lack of response. I go into tech problem solving mode and seem to try various methods of turning off the device, trying to trouble shoot why the device is not responding. It seems like a very extended process. In one dream I think I gave up, in the other I just kept trying, in both I woke up to find I had left my phone playing some extended podcast/whatever without a sleep timer and it was annoying me.

Not precognitive, indeed in some ways anti-precognitive. Seems to circumstantially support the received view.

possible future evidence

a successful theory would enable us to predict what someone reports as the content of their dreams from observation of their physiology during their sleep.

scientists might correct someones dream recollection - No you didnt dream that but the story they tell instead is not entirely alien to the dreamer - Suppose it recounted an adventure with some secretly loved acquaintance of [the dreamers], a person unknown to the researchers.

responses to discovering an alternative theory was true

Suppose we dicovered that the #cassette theory or the #production upon waking theory was true. Then it would turn out that wew had been under an illusion that we were dreaming while we slept. Malcolm views this as senseless (it breaks dreamers authority, for Wittgenstein breaks a criterion for having had a dream viz., saying that you did).

This is the standard solid_objects case

solutions to precognitive dreams

my view

The nonlinguistic representation system is both a expectation generating system (i.e. a simulator/prediction generator) it is also the recollection system or at least a key part of it (this is why we have semantic search/recollection which is the alchemists stone of search engines).

This fits beautifully with at least the following element of Dennett's view:

We interpret, extrapolate, revise; it sometimes seems that we "relive" the incidents and draw conclusions from this reliving-conclusions that are then expressed in what we actually compose then and there as our recollections.
(fp. 5)

He then asks:

(cont:)It is not easy to analyze what must be going on when this happens. What is the raw material, the evidence, the basis for these reconstructions we call recollections?

One suggestion - often but no always the initial prompt will be a sentence. (Better saved by effective digitisation). This may trigger a running of the simulation with the grossest features in place. Then further attentional/computation resources are invested in fleshing out the simulation. Other times it may be something similar that triggers recollection. Recollection is not sharply distinguished from imagination. At other times one may try to reverse engineer. The device of working out that someone/something/some detail had to be there. Right, it was my first coffee of the day, and I had got to work late and then using that to seed the neural simulation is often effective.

the traces are going to be stored in defusable connections/tendencies in the neural simulation component/components. These slight adjustments in the expectational generation connections will outlast the memory - they will become too diffuse to trigger a resimulation of the event, and at that point we call it forgotten.

So a number of features one might expect: There is no sharp line between recollection and invention.

  • at the time of recollection striving to remember may result in the construction of new details of the dream that were not experienced (or at least neurally represented) during REM sleep

During the dream details will be invented as one pays attention to them.

  • e.g. when one looks at a book one will be able to read it and it will have had text in it all along (it was not a blank book in which writing suddenly appeared) but there was no neural representation of that text before one looks.
  • In a dream one looks up from the table/whatever was occupying one's attention and suddenly recognises your great aunt/school friend/whomever. In a sense its utterly implausible that they could have sat down without your recognising them. The sequence goes as follows: you dream you are in a cafe, what do you do at a cafe? look out the window and drink coffee while there are people around. Brain runs low level undetailed simulation of people around you, and detailed simulation of what you are attending to - the scene outside the window and the smell of coffee. Your attention wanders to your surroundings in the cafe - there are people here - which people - ah simulation summons up
  • a person simulation in order to fulfill your previous belief that there are people here, but now you are attending to it it has to be a particular person - voila: your aunt.
