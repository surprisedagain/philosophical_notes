main

making a mistake is holding (or at least expressing) a false statement.

The Cartesian model of meaning took cause and representation to be the same. The relationship was too simple. and attempts to make it sophisticated didnt seem to help either.

The other half- the predictive half - was left out of the equation. (Indeed somewhat similar to trying to solve a equation that has too few variables to represent the situation, or trying to find the real roots of an equation that has only complex roots.

Then we introduce the predictive aspect in terms of falsification. But now our account of falsification is too simple. Focused upon individual sentences rather than whole theories.

objection

If we can enquire of our teachers both statements and their negations. (i.e. not just which sentences are true but also which ones are false. Then we do not need them to deny anything.

Still observing mistakes and correcting them seems to be a key component of langauges use. Can we make sense of this importance in wholistic dispositionalist terms? Does this help alleviate the anxiety that we just know which theory to hold true, and modify it without ever really being responsible to the theory we held yesterday?
