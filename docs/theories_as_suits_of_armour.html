theories as suits of armour

short

An analogy between theories and suits of armour.

main

Suppose there were suits of "armour" that did not merely protect the wearer from the blows and scrapes of external objects but offered a performance advantage. Suppose they enabled the wearer to jump slightly further, run slightly faster, or lift heavier loads. Or suppose that there were some quite unusual and rare but essential physical tasks that could only be performed by someone wearing such a suit (e.g. deep sea diving). Would you choose to wear such a suit then?

(Perhaps the suits only help with quite rarified tasks which only come up occasionally in day to day life. but the rare problems they do enable us to solve are so important that the people who solve them are feted as celebrities or heroes.)

Surely, the sensible approach would be to put on such a suit when it was required for a task at hand and take it off at other times. That way one would have the benefits the suit offered when they were acquired and wouldn't have to put up with the necessary discomfort and restrictions of wearing the suit at other times.

One might find a handful of obsessives who decide instead that they should live in these suits at all times (perhaps only reluctantly taking them off to go to the toilet or perhaps only with friends who do not like these suits) and insist that they can be made as comfortable and flexible as wearing nothing at all.

These obsessives might provide a benefit to other people. Their obsession might lead them to develop suits which provide less limitations on how they can bend their joints and move their bodies; suits closely mimicing the natural underlying movement of the muscles and joints. These people compete and collaborate to refine their suits of armour and they get pretty good but they are never quite achieve the freedom of movement of being armorless. (And in any case sometimes the obsessives will simply adjust their bodies to the suits and claim that this is the best way to live.)

One day the obsessives might be astonished to find that other people live quite happily with no armour at all. They might pity them and wonder how people could possibly be naked and happy!
