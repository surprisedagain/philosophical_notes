The core themes of Wittgenstein's early work are:

  • a positive account of our knowledge of necessary truths
  • the incoherence of attempts to talk directly about modality (and hence the supposed inability to give his account straightforwardly)
  • defending the claim that all necessary truths are BOTH part of the structure of language and part of the metaphysical structure of the world.

There are also the first hints of a suggestion of a recognition of under-determination though these are far more developed in his later works
